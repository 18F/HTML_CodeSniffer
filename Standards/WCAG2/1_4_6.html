WCAG 2.0 Success Criterion 1.4.6: Contrast (Enhanced) (Level AAA)

Back to: Home - Level AAA Summary

Definition

The visual presentation of text and images of text has a contrast ratio of at least 7:1, except for the following:

  • Large Text: Large-scale text and images of large-scale text have a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1;
  • Incidental: Text or images of text that are part of an inactive user interface component, that are pure decoration, that are not visible to anyone, or that are part of a picture that contains significant other visual content, have no contrast requirement.
  • Logotypes: Text that is part of a logo or brand name has no minimum contrast requirement.

More discussion from W3C: Understanding Success Criterion 1.4.6

Summary

This is a stricter version of Success Criterion 1.4.3. Normally a contrast ratio of 3.0:1 is a standard for normal users (ie. without loss of visual acuity, or 20/20).

The 4.5:1 contrast ratio (found in Success Criterion 1.4.3 for body text, and this one for large print) is chosen to cater for the loss of contrast recognition for users with approximately 20/40 vision typically suffer, while the 7.0:1 level quoted in this criterion is meant to handle users with approximately 20/80 vision. For those with worse vision (and cannot be corrected), it is suggested that those users would be better suited with assistive technology.

For means of comparison, the maximum contrast ratio (between white and black) is 21.0:1.
