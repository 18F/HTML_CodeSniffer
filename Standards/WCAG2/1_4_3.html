Success Criterion 1.4.3: Contrast (Minimum) (Level AA)

The visual presentation of text and images of text has a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1, except for the following:

  • Large Text: Large-scale text and images of large-scale text have a contrast ratio of at least 3:1;
  • Incidental: Text or images of text that are part of an inactive user interface component, that are pure decoration, that are not visible to anyone, or that are part of a picture that contains significant other visual content, have no contrast requirement.
  • Logotypes: Text that is part of a logo or brand name has no minimum contrast requirement.

(From Understanding WCAG 2.0: Understanding Success Criterion 1.4.3)

Note

Superseded by Success Criterion 1.4.6 at level AAA, which is a stricter application of the same concept.

Discussion

Summary

The Understanding page explains why the ratios of 3.0:1, 4.5:1 and 7.0:1 were chosen (the latter for the AAA standard, Success Criterion 1.4.6.

The 3.0:1 ratio is an ergonomic standard under ANSI and ISO (ISO 9241), and the 4.5:1 contrast ratio specified under AA is specified based on an empirical study that users with 20/40 visual acuity have a loss of contrast sensitivity of the equivalent of 1.5:1, compared to 20/20 vision - hence (3.0 * 1.5) = 4.5:1 contrast ratio.

For AAA level and Success Criterion 1.4.6, the contrast ratio specified (7.0:1) is supposed to satisfy 20/80 visual acuity (without correction or assistive technology) - about another 1.5x increase over the 4.5:1 ratio.

For means of comparison, the maximum contrast ratio, between white and black, is 21.0:1. A formula is available for calculating contrast ratio from two RGB values' "relative luminescence".
